Detailed Termite Inspections and Identification

The first crucial step in effective termite control is a comprehensive and accurate inspection. Subterranean Termite Identification and Inspection

Subterranean termites are the most common type of termite found in Bradenton, FL. They build mud tubes to travel between their underground colonies and their food source (your home). Our inspectors are experts at locating these mud tubes, even in hidden or hard-to-reach areas. They also look for signs of wood damage, such as hollow-sounding wood, blistering paint, or discarded wings. Identifying the specific species of subterranean termite is also vital, as different species may require slightly different treatment approaches.

Drywood Termite Identification and Inspection

Drywood termites live within the wood they infest and do not require contact with the soil. They often leave behind small piles of fecal pellets, known as frass, which resemble sawdust. Our inspectors are skilled in identifying frass and locating the small kick-out holes drywood termites create to expel their pellets. Detecting drywood termite infestations often requires specialized tools and a keen eye for detail, both of which our team possesses.

Liquid Termite Treatments

Liquid termiticides are a common and effective method for creating a protective barrier around your property. We use professional-grade, non-repellent liquid termiticides that are undetectable to termites. As termites tunnel through the treated soil, they pick up the termiticide and carry it back to their colony, effectively spreading the treatment and eliminating the entire colony. This method provides long-lasting protection around the perimeter of your home.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer a different approach to termite control. These systems utilize bait stations strategically placed around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting insect growth regulator that termites consume and carry back to their colony. This disrupts the termite's molting process, leading to the demise of the colony. Baiting systems are an excellent option for ongoing monitoring and control.

Localized Termite Treatments

For drywood termite infestations, localized treatments may be necessary. This can involve injecting termiticides directly into infested wood or utilizing fumigation in severe cases. Our technicians are skilled in applying these localized treatments precisely to eliminate drywood termite colonies without causing unnecessary disruption.

Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundation

Subterranean termites build mud tubes to protect themselves from predators and moisture as they travel from their underground colony to a food source (your home). These tubes are typically the width of a pencil and are found on exterior or interior walls, foundations, or around pipes. If you see these earthy tunnels, it's a strong indicator of subterranean termite activity. Don't disturb them, as this can cause the termites to simply reroute and continue their destructive path. Call a professional immediately for a thorough inspection and treatment plan to address this common sign of a termite problem. Ignoring mud tubes is like ignoring a ticking time bomb within your property's structure. Professional intervention is critical to stop the infestation in its tracks and prevent further damage to your home or business. Damaged or Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, often leaving the exterior shell intact. This means that wood that appears normal on the surface may be severely damaged underneath. Discarded Termite Wings

When termite colonies mature, they produce winged reproductive termites called swarmers. These swarmers leave the colony to find a mate and establish new colonies. After swarming, they shed their wings. Finding small piles of discarded wings, particularly near windowsills, doorways, or light sources, is a strong indicator of a nearby termite colony. These discarded wings resemble fish scales and are a clear signal that a termite swarm has occurred. Ignoring discarded wings means potentially overlooking the beginning of a new termite colony on your property or in your immediate vicinity. Swarming often occurs during warmer months after rainfall, so be particularly vigilant during these periods. If you discover discarded wings, it's crucial to contact a termite professional for an inspection immediately. This is a primary sign that indicates a termite problem and necessitates professional Termite Control in Bradenton, FL. Our team can assess whether the swarming originated from your property or a nearby location and implement the necessary preventative or treatment measures. Acting quickly when you find discarded wings can prevent an even larger termite problem from developing. Professional intervention is key to identifying the source of the swarm and implementing effective control measures to protect your property from future termite activity and the establishment of new colonies. This is a critical step in safeguarding your home or business from the destructive potential of termites and maintaining the structural integrity of your property. Frass (Termite Droppings)

Drywood termites produce small, hard, pellet-like fecal droppings called frass. These pellets are often found in small piles near infested wood, resembling sawdust. The color of the frass can vary depending on the color of the wood the termites are consuming. Finding these tiny, six-sided pellets is a clear sign of drywood termite activity within the structure. Ignoring frass means allowing drywood termites to continue their damaging work within your walls or other wooden structures. These pellets are a direct byproduct of their wood consumption and are a visible indicator of an active drywood termite infestation. If you notice small piles of what looks like sawdust, particularly in areas where there are no visible signs of sawing or drilling, it's highly likely to be frass.
